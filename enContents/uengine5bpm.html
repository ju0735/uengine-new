Our Values

Why did uEngine open-source its BPM product?

The uEngine BPM project offers BPMS products, which were traditionally accessible only through expensive licensing fees, in an open-source format. Alongside this, it provides education and consulting services to facilitate faithful education and internalization through a consulting-cost-centered budget structure. It also allows faithful customization focused on customer requirements through the provision of source code and enables multiple service channels. Among the considerations for BPM adoption, the technical aspect pertains to how easily it can be customized and seamlessly integrated into our organizational system environment without imposing a burden. To facilitate customization and low-risk integration, the architecture of uEngine BPMS adopts the approach of IoC (Inversion of Control), which is an aspect of the "object-oriented component framework," and the concept of "design reuse." This approach provides a boundary that refines BPM Hot-spot services (fixed parts) and BPM customization frequent elements (variable parts) by offering component interfaces such as separating organization lookup logic, component interface of activity types, and listeners for events occurring in the engine. It serves as a fixed part service, offering functionalities such as automatic generation of activity component UI through reflection, management of instance data, MQ processing, OLAP analysis, etc., thereby enabling developers to easily customize BPM-based development without any concerns about the underlying BPM technology. This approach has adapted approaches from various frameworks like Spring Framework into the realm of BPM-based system development. It significantly reduces the risks associated with BPM products, which typically offer package-based solutions and only allow server/client-based integration, thereby maximizing availability.
